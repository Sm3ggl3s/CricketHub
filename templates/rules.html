Rules of Cricket

General Description of the Rules

The rules of cricket are primarily considered as the "laws of cricket."

Cricket is played with a bat and ball.

There are two teams of 11 each in a cricket match. One team bowls with the ball and the other team bats.

Test Cricket is played over the maximum course of five days with white jerseys. Considered by most as the purest form of the game, its fame lies in the ambitious, disciplined, and determined nature of those who seek to master it.

Unlike the other formats, each team can have two innings in Test Cricket with a maximum of four innings per match.

There are no bowling restrictions in Test cricket. Bowlers can bowl for however many overs they wish to bowl.
